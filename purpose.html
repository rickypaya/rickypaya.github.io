Purpose Statement

The state of the American political climate, corporate greed, and the pervasive lack of integrity in those who hold positions of leadership continue to shock and dismay those who witness their true extent. With each passing day, we witness greater injustices inflicted upon innocent lives, while hatred and greed are allowed to flourish unchecked. This normalization of violence and exploitation has desensitized many in our society, perpetuating a cycle of oppression and inequality. I firmly believe that technology corporations are complicit in perpetuating these injustices.

In an ideal world, technology companies would prioritize the needs and well-being of their users above all else. However, we are forced to grapple with questions about who is at the helm of these companies, what their business models prioritize, and why. All too often, the answers reveal a pattern of exploitation and disregard for the working class, masquerading behind the guise of innovation and progress.

As a software engineer, I have been complicit in this system. I have worked for companies that prioritize profit over people, selling software at exorbitant prices while claiming to benefit their users. I have seen firsthand the countless hours of labor poured into products that ultimately serve the interests of a wealthy few, while leaving the majority behind, and with those same profits, contribute to war and genocide.

But I refuse to be a cog in this machine. I refuse to contribute to a system that prioritizes profits over people, that perpetuates inequality and exploitation. Instead, I aspire to become a leader in the software industry who champions ethical practices, prioritizes the well-being of users and partners alike, and works towards a future where technology truly serves the greater good. I aspire to be a technology leader who establishes software systems that can continue to flourish independently without the need for powerful oligarchs and contribute back to the community it is intended for. Though I aspire to be a leader, I do not aspire to work for a company, I refuse to be another number; a dispensible worker, throwing their lives away for the sake of making money to support themselves. There is more to life than devoting all of one's time to making someone else profit. I want to be empowered as an entreprenur, as a freelance software developer and creative, to solve to problems of those who I care about using the tools at my disposal to create meaningful work and support and give back to the communities that brought me where I am today. I want to inspire others to feel empowered, to break out of their routines, to take a chance, to make a change.

I envision a future where technology empowers individuals and communities, where innovation is driven by a commitment to justice and equity. As a software leader, I will strive to challenge the status quo, to dismantle systems of oppression within the industry, and to advocate for a more ethical and humane approach to technology.
